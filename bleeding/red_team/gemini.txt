### 1. The SIMD Boundary Trap (The 32-Byte Alignment Nightmare)

Your engine compiles with `-mavx2` and uses hand-vectorized kernels to speed up matrix multiplication and activations.

* **The Looming Bug:** AVX2 double-precision intrinsic instructions like `_mm256_load_pd` expect data to be strictly **32-byte aligned** in physical memory. If your custom arena allocator (`lancius_arena.c`) or your memory liveness planner serves up a tensor buffer offset that is *even slightly* misaligned (e.g., aligned to 8 or 16 bytes), your CPU will immediately throw a hardware-level **General Protection Fault (GPF)** and crash the process instantly with a segfault.
* **The `v10A3` Stress Test:** 1. Hunt down every vector load in your kernel files. If you cannot absolutely guarantee 32-byte boundary alignment on every slice of your arena, force your Blue Team (Qwen) to swap out `_mm256_load_pd` for its unaligned counterpart, `_mm256_loadu_pd`. It incurs a negligible clock-cycle penalty on modern Intel/AMD architectures but completely eliminates alignment-related fatal crashes.
2. Enforce alignment in your allocator: Ensure your arena offset rounding macro strictly pads allocations to multiples of 32 bytes:
```c
#define ALIGN_32(size) (((size) + 31) & ~31)

```

### 2. The Cyclic Graph Stack Overflow (The Silent Malformed Payload Killer)

Your topological wave scheduler (`lancius_scheduler.c`) and symbolic autodiff engine (`lancius_autodiff.c`) recursively traverse your SSA graph to establish execution order and compute gradients.

* **The Looming Bug:** If a user passes your compiler an ONNX model that contains a feedback loop, or if a corrupted binary file inadvertently points a node's dependency back to itself, your recursive depth-first search (DFS) will enter an infinite loop. Within microseconds, the C call stack will overflow, terminating the execution with zero diagnostic logs or error output.
* **The `v10A3` Stress Test:** Before allowing the compiler to schedule a single wave, implement a strict **Tarjan’s strongly connected components algorithm** or a simple cycle detection pass (using node coloring: *unvisited, visiting, visited*). If a cycle is detected, halt execution immediately with a loud, descriptive trace: `[Lancius VM Exception] Cyclic dependency detected between Node X and Node Y. Aborting.`

---

### 3. The Exponential Underflow / Infinity Explosion

In your activation kernels (like SwiGLU or Sigmoid), you compute exponentials over arbitrary raw weight values using functions like `exp(-g)`.

* **The Looming Bug:** During the early steps of training or during high-variance inference passes, the input value `g` can occasionally spike. If `g` becomes a large negative number (e.g., `-800`), `exp(-g)` becomes `exp(800)`, which instantly overflows double-precision limits and evaluates to positive `Infinity`. Conversely, if it spikes positively, it underflows to `0.0`, resulting in division-by-zero errors in your backward gradient passes.
* **The `v10A3` Stress Test:** Implement numerical clamping (clipping) inside your mathematical activation functions. Guard your transcendental operators with safety envelopes:
```c
double safe_g = (g > 50.0) ? 50.0 : ((g < -50.0) ? -50.0 : g);

```


This tiny, high-performance branch guard protects your symbolic autograd from executing operations on infinity values and ensures your loss tracking never degrades into `NaN` under extreme inputs.
